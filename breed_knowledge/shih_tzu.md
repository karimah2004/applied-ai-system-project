# Shih Tzu

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 20–30 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: Low — leisurely walks and indoor play
- Brachycephalic: avoid heat, humidity, and intense exercise

## Common Health Issues
- Brachycephalic obstructive airway syndrome (BOAS): noisy breathing, exercise intolerance
- Eye problems: prominent eyes prone to scratches, ulcers, dryness — keep hair out of eyes
- Intervertebral disc disease (IVDD): back pain, hind weakness
- Dental disease: very prone due to small, crowded mouth
- Hip dysplasia: limping, difficulty rising
- Warning signs: open-mouth breathing, eye cloudiness/redness, dragging hind legs

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: ½ cup dry kibble split into 2 meals
- Puppy: 3 small meals daily
- Senior (10+): senior small-breed formula
- Restrictions: no grapes, raisins, onions, chocolate, xylitol

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: daily (long coat mats quickly)
- Professional grooming or trimming: every 6–8 weeks
- Face cleaning: daily — wipe eyes and muzzle area
- Bathing: every 3–4 weeks
- Nail trimming: every 3 weeks
- Teeth brushing: daily (critical for this breed)

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Affectionate, playful, and outgoing
- Mild stubborn streak — patient positive reinforcement works
- Excellent apartment dog; does not need outdoor space
- Good with children and other pets
- Loves attention; does not tolerate long periods alone

## Age-Specific Care
- Kitten/Puppy (0–1 year): start grooming early, 3 meals/day, socialize gently
- Adult (1–10 years): daily grooming, annual vet + dental, short walks
- Senior (10+ years): bi-annual vet, softer bedding, easier-to-chew food, daily face cleaning
