# Labrador Retriever

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 60–80 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: High — running, fetch, swimming, trail hiking
- Puppies (<1 year): 5 minutes per month of age twice daily; avoid hard surfaces

## Common Health Issues
- Hip and elbow dysplasia: limping, stiffness after rest
- Obesity: extremely food-motivated breed — monitor weight closely
- Exercise-induced collapse (EIC): sudden weakness after intense exercise; rest immediately
- Progressive retinal atrophy (PRA): night blindness, dilated pupils
- Warning signs: collapse during exercise, sudden vision changes, persistent limping

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: 2.5–3.5 cups dry kibble split into 2 meals; adjust for activity level
- Puppy: 3 meals daily, large-breed puppy formula
- Senior (7+): senior formula with joint support, smaller portions
- Restrictions: Labs are prone to overeating — use a slow feeder bowl
- No grapes, raisins, chocolate, xylitol, onions

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: weekly; twice weekly during shedding seasons
- Bathing: every 4–6 weeks; water-repellent coat dries quickly
- Ear checks: weekly — floppy ears prone to infections
- Nail trimming: every 3–4 weeks
- Teeth brushing: 2–3 times per week

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Highly trainable, one of the easiest breeds to train
- Positive reinforcement works best; food rewards are very effective
- Friendly with strangers, children, and other pets
- High energy as puppies — needs mental stimulation (puzzle toys, training sessions)
- Can become destructive if under-exercised

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): socialization priority, controlled exercise, 3 meals/day
- Adult (1–7 years): daily vigorous exercise, annual vet, dental care
- Senior (7+ years): bi-annual vet visits, weight management critical, gentle exercise
