# Golden Retriever

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 60–90 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: High — vigorous walks, fetch, swimming, off-leash play
- Puppies (<1 year): 5 minutes per month of age, twice daily to protect joints

## Common Health Issues
- Hip dysplasia: watch for limping, reluctance to rise, bunny-hopping gait
- Elbow dysplasia: front-leg lameness after exercise
- Cancer (high breed risk): unexplained lumps, weight loss, lethargy — see vet immediately
- Hypothyroidism: weight gain, hair loss, cold intolerance
- Heart disease (subvalvular aortic stenosis): fainting, exercise intolerance
- Warning signs requiring vet visit: persistent limping, rapid breathing at rest, sudden weight change

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult (1–7 years): 2–3 cups high-quality dry kibble, split into 2 meals daily
- Puppy: 3–4 meals daily; puppy-specific large-breed formula
- Senior (7+ years): lower-calorie senior formula, same 2-meal schedule
- Restrictions: avoid grapes, raisins, chocolate, xylitol, onions, macadamia nuts
- Obesity risk is high — measure portions, limit treats to <10% of daily calories

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: 2–3 times per week; daily during shedding seasons (spring/fall)
- Bathing: every 6–8 weeks or when dirty
- Ear checks: weekly — floppy ears trap moisture and are prone to infection
- Nail trimming: every 3–4 weeks
- Teeth brushing: 2–3 times per week minimum

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Highly intelligent, eager to please — responds well to positive reinforcement
- Gentle, friendly, tolerant of children and other pets
- Can develop separation anxiety if left alone for long periods
- Prone to mouthing/chewing as puppies — provide chew toys
- Basic obedience training should start by 8 weeks; puppy classes recommended

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): socialization critical, joint-protection exercise limits, 3–4 meals/day
- Adult (1–7 years): consistent daily exercise, annual vet check, dental hygiene
- Senior (7+ years): bi-annual vet visits, joint supplements (glucosamine), softer bedding, reduced-intensity exercise
