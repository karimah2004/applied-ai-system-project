# German Shepherd

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 90–120 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: High — running, hiking, fetch, agility, scent work
- Working breed with very high energy; under-exercise leads to destructive behavior

## Common Health Issues
- Hip and elbow dysplasia: limping, bunny-hopping, reluctance to exercise
- Degenerative myelopathy: progressive hind-end weakness — common in seniors
- Bloat (GDV): distended abdomen, unproductive retching = immediate emergency
- Exocrine pancreatic insufficiency (EPI): weight loss despite good appetite, loose stools
- Warning signs: sudden hind-end collapse, bloated belly with retching — both are emergencies

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: 3–4 cups high-quality dry kibble split into 2 meals
- Elevated bowl and no exercise 1 hour before/after meals (bloat risk)
- Puppy: 3–4 meals daily, large-breed puppy formula
- Senior (8+): senior/joint-support formula
- Restrictions: no grapes, onions, chocolate, xylitol

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: 3–4 times per week; daily during shedding seasons ("blowing coat")
- Bathing: every 6–8 weeks
- Ear checks: weekly
- Nail trimming: every 3–4 weeks
- Teeth brushing: 2–3 times per week

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Highly intelligent, loyal, and protective — one of the most trainable breeds
- Requires consistent, firm positive reinforcement
- Early socialization critical to prevent over-protectiveness
- Excels at obedience, protection, search and rescue, and service work
- Needs a job or structured activity to stay mentally satisfied

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): socialization priority, joint-protection exercise limits, 3–4 meals/day
- Adult (1–8 years): 90+ min daily exercise, mental work, annual vet, dental care
- Senior (8+ years): bi-annual vet, hind-end monitoring, joint supplements, ramps for access
