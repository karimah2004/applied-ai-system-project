# Corgi (Pembroke Welsh Corgi)

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 45–60 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: Moderate to high — walks, fetch, agility; avoid excessive jumping (spinal risk)
- Despite short legs, Corgis are herding dogs with substantial endurance

## Common Health Issues
- Intervertebral disc disease (IVDD): back pain, hind weakness — minimize jumping on/off furniture
- Hip dysplasia: limping, difficulty rising
- Progressive retinal atrophy (PRA): night blindness
- Degenerative myelopathy: progressive hind-end weakness in seniors
- Obesity: prone due to food motivation — strict portion control
- Warning signs: dragging hind legs, sudden vision changes, persistent limping

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: 0.75–1.5 cups dry kibble split into 2 meals (adjust for activity)
- Puppy: 3 meals daily, small-breed puppy formula
- Senior (9+): senior formula, reduced calories
- Restrictions: prone to obesity — no free feeding; limit treats; avoid grapes, onions, chocolate

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: 2–3 times per week; daily during shedding seasons (heavy shedder)
- Bathing: every 6–8 weeks
- Nail trimming: every 3–4 weeks
- Ear checks: monthly
- Teeth brushing: 2–3 times per week

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Highly intelligent and eager to please — one of the easiest herding breeds to train
- Strong herding instinct: may nip at heels of children or other pets
- Positive reinforcement; food rewards work very well
- Can be vocal (barking) — teach "quiet" command early
- Excellent for agility and obedience competitions

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): ramps to prevent jumping, 3 meals/day, early socialization
- Adult (1–9 years): daily exercise, annual vet, dental hygiene
- Senior (9+ years): bi-annual vet, spinal monitoring, softer bedding, ramps everywhere
