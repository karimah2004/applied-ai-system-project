# Beagle

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 45–60 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: Moderate — walks, sniff walks, fetch; always on leash or in fenced yard (strong prey drive)
- Scent hound: nose leads the way — leash is non-negotiable outdoors

## Common Health Issues
- Obesity: extremely food-motivated; prone to overeating
- Intervertebral disc disease (IVDD): back pain, hind weakness
- Epilepsy: seizures (genetic in the breed)
- Hypothyroidism: weight gain, lethargy, coat changes
- Cherry eye: pink protrusion at inner eye corner
- Warning signs: seizure activity, sudden hind-end weakness, unexplained weight gain

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: 1–1.5 cups dry kibble split into 2 meals
- Puppy: 3 meals daily
- Senior (10+): senior formula with reduced calories
- Restrictions: strict portion control — Beagles will eat until sick; secure trash cans
- No grapes, raisins, onions, chocolate, xylitol

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: weekly
- Bathing: every 4–6 weeks (can develop "hound smell")
- Ear checks: weekly — long floppy ears trap moisture; prone to infections
- Nail trimming: every 3–4 weeks
- Teeth brushing: 2–3 times per week

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Friendly, curious, merry — great family dogs
- Training can be challenging: easily distracted by scent
- Positive reinforcement with food rewards works well
- Prone to howling — teach "quiet" early; be considerate of neighbors
- Pack-oriented; does well with other dogs

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): 3 meals/day, leash training priority, socialization
- Adult (1–10 years): daily exercise with scent enrichment, annual vet
- Senior (10+ years): bi-annual vet, weight management critical, softer bedding
