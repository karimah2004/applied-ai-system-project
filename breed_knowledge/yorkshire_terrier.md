# Yorkshire Terrier

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 30 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: Low to moderate — short walks, indoor play
- Small size means indoor play can supplement outdoor walks

## Common Health Issues
- Tracheal collapse: honking cough — use harness, never neck collar
- Hypoglycemia (especially puppies): weakness, trembling, seizures — keep glucose handy
- Patellar luxation: intermittent skipping/limping on hind leg
- Portosystemic shunt: stunted growth, neurological signs in puppies
- Dental disease: severe overcrowding in tiny mouth
- Warning signs: honking cough = vet soon; seizures, trembling = vet immediately

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: ¼–½ cup dry kibble split into 2–3 meals (small stomach)
- Puppy: 3–4 small meals daily to prevent hypoglycemia
- Senior (10+): senior small-breed formula
- Restrictions: no grapes, raisins, chocolate, xylitol, onions
- Small-breed kibble recommended for tiny mouths

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: daily (long silky coat tangles easily)
- Professional grooming or trimming: every 6–8 weeks
- Bathing: every 3–4 weeks
- Nail trimming: every 2–3 weeks
- Teeth brushing: daily — Yorkshire Terriers have severe dental disease risk
- Ear checks: monthly

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Bold, feisty, and confident despite tiny size
- Can be stubborn — consistent positive reinforcement required
- Prone to "small dog syndrome" without boundaries
- Good watchdog but can be yappy — teach "quiet" early
- Generally better with older children (rough play can injure them)

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): 4 small meals/day (hypoglycemia prevention), harness from day one
- Adult (1–10 years): daily grooming, annual vet + dental, short daily walks
- Senior (10+ years): bi-annual vet, dental extractions common, warmth (no cold tolerance), gentle play
