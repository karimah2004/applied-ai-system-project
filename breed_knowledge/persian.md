# Persian (Cat)

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 15–20 minutes of gentle play per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: Low — calm, sedentary breed; prefers gentle play over active chasing
- Brachycephalic: avoid overheating and stress

## Common Health Issues
- Brachycephalic airway syndrome: noisy breathing, exercise intolerance
- Polycystic kidney disease (PKD): increased thirst/urination, weight loss
- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM): labored breathing = emergency
- Eye discharge and epiphora: constant tearing due to flat face — requires daily cleaning
- Dental malocclusion: teeth overcrowded due to flat face — dental disease common
- Warning signs: open-mouth breathing = emergency; blood in urine, excessive thirst

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: ¼–½ cup dry kibble or 2 oz wet food, 2 meals daily
- Wet food encouraged — prone to kidney disease and needs hydration
- Senior (10+): kidney-support formula
- Restrictions: no onions, garlic, grapes, chocolate, xylitol

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: daily — long coat mats severely without daily grooming
- Professional grooming: every 6–8 weeks
- Facial cleaning: daily — wipe eye discharge and facial folds
- Bathing: monthly (Persians can get greasy coat)
- Nail trimming: every 2–3 weeks
- Teeth brushing: daily (high dental disease risk)

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Calm, gentle, and quiet — ideal apartment cat
- Not very trainable or active; prefers laps to tricks
- Sensitive to loud or chaotic environments
- Bonds deeply with owner; not good with being ignored
- Not a climber — prefers floor-level and sofa-level spaces

## Age-Specific Care
- Kitten (0–1 year): start daily grooming routine immediately; 3 small meals/day
- Adult (1–10 years): daily brushing and face cleaning, annual vet + dental check
- Senior (10+ years): bi-annual vet, kidney bloodwork, soft bedding, assisted grooming if arthritic
