# Bulldog (English Bulldog)

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 20–30 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: Low — gentle walks only; NO intense running, NO vigorous play
- Brachycephalic breed: highly susceptible to overheating
- Exercise in cool weather only; watch for heavy panting, stumbling

## Common Health Issues
- Brachycephalic obstructive airway syndrome: snoring, blue gums = emergency
- Hip dysplasia and joint problems: limping, reluctance to move
- Skin fold infections: redness, odor, discharge in wrinkles
- Cherry eye and eye irritation: protruding pink mass at eye corner
- Heart problems: exercise intolerance, coughing
- Warning signs: gasping for breath, blue/purple gums, sudden collapse — call vet immediately

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: 1–1.5 cups dry kibble split into 2 meals
- Puppy: 3 small meals daily
- Senior (6+): senior formula, strict portion control
- Restrictions: highly prone to obesity — no free feeding; measure every meal
- Avoid gas-producing foods

## Grooming Requirements
- Wrinkle/fold cleaning: daily with damp cloth — bacteria thrive in warm folds
- Brushing: weekly
- Bathing: every 4–6 weeks
- Tail pocket cleaning: weekly
- Nail trimming: every 3 weeks (less active, nails grow faster)
- Teeth brushing: 3 times per week

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Gentle, calm, and loyal
- Stubborn and independent — training requires patience and consistency
- Short training sessions (5–10 minutes) with high-value treats
- Generally friendly with children but supervise interactions due to size
- Low energy — content with lounging

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): no stairs, no jumping, 3 small meals, close breathing monitoring
- Adult (1–6 years): short daily walks, daily fold cleaning, annual vet
- Senior (6+ years): bi-annual vet, joint supplements, ramp for furniture access
