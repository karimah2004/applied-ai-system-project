# Siamese (Cat)

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 20–30 minutes of active play per day
- Frequency: Daily (they will demand it)
- Intensity: Moderate to high for a cat — interactive toys, puzzle feeders, wand play
- Very active and vocal; boredom leads to destructive behavior

## Common Health Issues
- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM): labored breathing, lethargy — vet emergency
- Progressive retinal atrophy (PRA): night blindness
- Amyloidosis: liver disease; weight loss, jaundice
- Respiratory infections (FHV, FCV): sneezing, eye/nasal discharge — Siamese prone
- Warning signs: open-mouth breathing = always emergency; persistent sneezing with discharge

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: ¼–½ cup dry kibble or 2 oz wet food, 2 meals daily
- Slim build natural — don't overfeed
- Senior (10+): senior formula with kidney support
- Fresh water always available; consider cat fountain
- Restrictions: no onions, garlic, grapes, chocolate, xylitol

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: weekly (short coat, low maintenance)
- Bathing: rarely needed
- Ear checks: monthly
- Nail trimming: every 2–3 weeks
- Teeth brushing: 2–3 times per week (prone to periodontal disease)

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Extremely vocal — will carry on conversations; not for noise-sensitive owners
- Very social and people-oriented; suffers from loneliness — consider getting two
- Highly trainable: can learn leash walking, commands, and tricks
- Sensitive to changes in routine; can develop stress-related illness
- Excellent with children and cat-friendly dogs

## Age-Specific Care
- Kitten (0–1 year): 3–4 small meals/day, socialization, interactive play critical
- Adult (1–10 years): daily active play, annual vet, dental care
- Senior (10+ years): bi-annual vet, HCM/kidney screening, gentle play
