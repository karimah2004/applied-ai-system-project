# French Bulldog

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 20–30 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: Low to moderate — short walks, light play; NEVER intense exercise
- Brachycephalic breed: overheating and breathing difficulties are serious risks
- Avoid exercise in heat or humidity; early morning or evening only in summer

## Common Health Issues
- Brachycephalic obstructive airway syndrome (BOAS): snoring, labored breathing, blue-tinged gums = emergency
- Intervertebral disc disease (IVDD): back pain, hind-leg weakness — avoid jumping
- Hip dysplasia: limping, difficulty rising
- Skin fold dermatitis: redness, odor in facial/tail folds
- Eye problems (cherry eye, corneal ulcers): redness, squinting, discharge
- Warning signs: struggling to breathe, sudden paralysis, persistent eye discharge

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: 1–1.5 cups dry kibble, 2 meals daily
- Puppy: 3 small meals daily; puppy formula for small breeds
- Senior (7+): senior formula, monitor weight carefully
- Restrictions: prone to obesity — strict portion control; no table scraps
- Avoid gas-producing foods (broccoli, beans); Frenchies are already gassy

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: weekly with soft brush
- Facial fold cleaning: daily with damp cloth to prevent infection
- Bathing: every 4–6 weeks
- Tail pocket cleaning: check and clean weekly
- Nail trimming: every 3–4 weeks
- Teeth brushing: 2–3 times per week

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Affectionate, playful, and adaptable to apartment living
- Stubborn streak — keep training sessions short (5–10 minutes) and fun
- Responds to positive reinforcement; avoid harsh corrections
- Tends to bond strongly with one person; can develop separation anxiety
- Generally good with children and other pets

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): avoid stairs and jumping (spinal risk), 3 meals/day, puppy-proof home
- Adult (1–7 years): short daily walks, annual vet, fold cleaning routine
- Senior (7+ years): bi-annual vet visits, soft bedding, ramp instead of stairs, weight management
