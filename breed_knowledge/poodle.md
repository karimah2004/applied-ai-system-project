# Poodle (Standard)

## Exercise Needs
- Duration: 60 minutes per day
- Frequency: Daily
- Intensity: Moderate to high — walks, swimming, agility, fetch
- Highly athletic and one of the most intelligent breeds

## Common Health Issues
- Hip dysplasia: limping, stiffness
- Bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus): distended abdomen, unproductive retching = emergency
- Addison's disease: lethargy, vomiting, collapse during stress
- Progressive retinal atrophy (PRA): night blindness
- Sebaceous adenitis: skin scaling, hair loss, musty odor
- Warning signs: bloated abdomen + retching = immediate vet emergency

## Dietary Guidelines
- Adult: 1.5–3 cups dry kibble split into 2 meals (based on size/activity)
- Feed from elevated bowl and avoid exercise 1 hour before/after meals (bloat prevention)
- Puppy: 3 meals daily
- Senior (9+): senior formula, monitor weight
- Restrictions: no grapes, raisins, onions, chocolate, xylitol

## Grooming Requirements
- Brushing: daily to prevent matting (non-shedding coat grows continuously)
- Professional grooming: every 6–8 weeks
- Ear checks: weekly — prone to ear infections (hair grows in ear canal)
- Nail trimming: every 3–4 weeks
- Teeth brushing: daily (prone to dental disease)

## Temperament and Training Notes
- Extremely intelligent — learns commands quickly, excels at complex tricks
- Positive reinforcement; bores easily with repetition — vary training activities
- Sensitive to harsh corrections
- Sociable and friendly; good with children and other pets
- Needs mental stimulation as much as physical exercise — puzzle toys, nose work

## Age-Specific Care
- Puppy (0–1 year): early socialization, 3 meals/day, start grooming routine early
- Adult (1–9 years): daily exercise + mental enrichment, annual vet, regular grooming
- Senior (9+ years): bi-annual vet, softer exercise (swimming), dental care critical
